Part 1: Copilot Chat — The Basics

▾
💡 Why This Matters: Every AI interaction starts with a prompt — the instruction you give Copilot. Milling and manufacturing professionals who master prompting can transform how they track production yields, manage supply chains, prepare quality reports, and communicate across 40+ community mills.

🧬 Anatomy of a Great Prompt

Every effective prompt includes up to four components. You don’t always need all four, but the more context you provide, the better Copilot’s response:

Goal What do you want Copilot to do? (e.g., “Summarize…”, “Draft…”, “Analyze…”) Context Background or role info (e.g., “I work in milling operations…”, “For our S&OP review…”) Scope Boundaries and limits (e.g., “From the last 7 days…”, “Only from our Great Plains region…”) Format How should the output look? (e.g., “In a table…”, “As bullet points…”, “In 3 paragraphs…”)
Remember: You can always iterate! If the first result isn’t perfect, refine your prompt and try again. Think of it as a conversation, not a single command.

Practice 1: Your First Prompt — Catch Up on Your Day

  1. Open a new browser tab and navigate to M365copilot.com
  2. Make sure you see “Work” mode enabled (this lets Copilot search your Microsoft 365 data)
  3. In the prompt window, type the following:
Copilot, summarize my emails, Teams messages, and channel messages from the last 1 workday. Categorize them as internal operations, supply chain updates, customer requests, or messages from my team or leadership, and prioritize them for my attention. List action items in a dedicated column and suggest follow-ups if possible, in a dedicated column. The table should look like this: Type (Mail/Teams/Channel) | Topic | Summarization | Category | Priority | Action Item | Follow-up. If I have been directly mentioned, make the font of the topic bold.
🔍 What just happened? Copilot searched your Microsoft 365 data — emails, Teams chats, and channel messages — and organized everything into a prioritized table. This is Copilot working in “Work” mode, grounded in your organizational data.

Practice 2: Save a Milling-Specific Prompt

Saving prompts lets you reuse them daily without rewriting. Let’s create one tailored to Ardent Mills operations:

  1. In Copilot Chat, type and run the following prompt:
Summarize all emails and Teams messages I received this week related to production schedules, wheat procurement, flour quality, food safety, customer orders, or equipment maintenance. Highlight any action items, deadlines, or pending approvals I need to address. Present results in a table: Source (Email/Teams) | From | Topic | Summary | Priority | Action Required | Deadline.
  1. Save the prompt: Hover over your prompt → click Save Prompt → name it something clear like “Weekly Milling Operations Catch-Up”
  2. Access saved prompts anytime: Open Copilot → click See more → select Prompt Gallery → go to Your prompts
  • Use clear names for prompts (e.g., “Weekly Milling Ops Catch-Up”, “Supply Chain Status Check”)
  • Share your most useful prompts with operations teams across facilities for consistency
  • Regularly review and update saved prompts to keep them relevant

Practice 3 (Optional): Memory & Custom Instructions

Copilot Memory creates a personalized experience based on your previous chats, work profile, and custom instructions. This helps Copilot understand your role at Ardent Mills.

  1. In Copilot, click the “…” menu
  2. Open Settings → select Personalization → then Custom Instructions
  3. Add instructions that reflect your role. Here’s an example:
I work in operations at Ardent Mills, North America's largest flour milling and grain ingredient company. We operate 40+ community mills across the U.S., Canada, and Puerto Rico. When I ask about production or supply chain topics, assume I understand milling terminology (extraction rates, ash content, protein levels, moisture content, etc.). When drafting communications, keep the tone professional and concise. Default to weekly timeframes unless I specify otherwise. Always include data sources when citing operational figures.

Part 2: Copilot in Outlook — Draft Operations Communications

▾
💡 Why This Matters: Ardent Mills teams communicate across 40+ facilities daily — wheat procurement updates, production schedule changes, customer order confirmations, food safety alerts, and quality reports. Copilot can draft these in seconds, letting you focus on the decisions rather than the drafting.

Draft a Supply Chain Update Email

  1. Open Outlook (web or desktop application)
  2. Select New Email
  3. Select Copilot in the ribbon
  4. From the drop-down menu, choose Draft with Copilot
  5. In the “What do you want this email to say?” prompt window, type:
Draft an email to the operations leadership team summarizing this week's wheat procurement and production status. Hard red winter wheat prices have increased 4% week-over-week due to drought conditions in the Southern Plains. Our Commerce City facility is running at 92% capacity with strong demand for our whole wheat flour lines. The Hastings mill completed its scheduled maintenance shutdown and is back online ahead of schedule. Two customer orders for organic flour blends are at risk of delay due to a railcar shortage affecting our Great Plains logistics corridor. Request that regional operations managers provide updated production forecasts by end of week. Include a reminder that the monthly S&OP review is next Tuesday. Keep the tone professional but collaborative.
  1. Review the generated draft
  2. Use the Adjust feature to modify tone, length, or level of detail
  3. Edit specific numbers or facility names to match your actual data before sending
💡 Tip: You can refine the email further with follow-up prompts:
Make the tone more urgent and add a sentence about the importance of maintaining on-time delivery commitments during peak baking season.
Shorten this email to 3 concise paragraphs and add a bullet-point summary of the key risks at the top.

Optional: Additional Operations Email Prompts

Try these other common Ardent Mills email scenarios:

Draft an email to the quality assurance team providing an update on food safety compliance across our facilities. Three of five scheduled system audits are complete. The remaining two mills are on track for completion by end of month. Highlight any findings that need corrective action and request a 15-minute check-in if needed.
Draft an email to customer accounts requesting updated Q3 volume forecasts by next Friday. Emphasize the importance of accuracy for production planning and wheat procurement scheduling. Include a brief reminder about our new specialty grain product lines that may support their reformulation needs.

Part 3: Copilot in Teams — Meetings & Conversations

▾
💡 Why This Matters: From S&OP reviews and production stand-ups to quality audits and customer innovation sessions — Ardent Mills teams spend significant time in meetings across 40+ locations. Copilot can capture decisions, action items, and critical production figures so you can focus on contributing rather than note-taking.

A. Meeting Recap

After an operations meeting (or during a live meeting with transcription enabled), use Copilot to get an instant summary:

  1. Open a recent Teams meeting that had transcription or recording enabled
  2. In the meeting chat, click the Copilot icon
  3. Enter the following prompt:
Summarize the key decisions, action items, and operational metrics discussed in this meeting. Organize by topic and highlight any deadlines or owners assigned. Flag anything specifically mentioned about: - Production yield or extraction rates - Wheat quality or procurement pricing - Customer orders or delivery timelines - Food safety or quality system updates - Equipment maintenance or capital projects Then list any action items assigned to my team.

You can follow up with more specific questions:

What were the main concerns raised about wheat supply or commodity pricing? Who committed to follow-up actions?
List all production figures and operational metrics mentioned in this meeting in a table format.

B. Copilot in Teams Chat & Channels

Use Copilot to catch up on conversations happening in your operations channels:

  1. Open a Teams chat or channel where operations discussions happen
  2. Click the Copilot icon in the chat
  3. Enter the following prompt:
What updates have been shared about production schedules, equipment status, or quality issues in the last 7 days? Summarize any blockers, open items, or decisions made.
🎤 Instructor Note: Recap requires transcription. If a meeting wasn’t recorded or transcribed, Copilot can’t recap it. Encourage organizers to enable transcription for S&OP reviews and production stand-ups.

Part 4: Analyst Agent — Production Data & Cost Modeling

▾
💡 Why This Matters: Milling operations generate massive amounts of data — production yields, extraction rates, energy consumption, wheat quality metrics, and cost variances across 40+ facilities. The Analyst agent can analyze this data, build models, and generate visualizations instantly — even if you don’t write code.

What is Analyst?

Analyst is an expert agent built into the Copilot app. It can perform advanced data analysis and Python-powered computations — turning your milling operations data into actionable insights with charts and models in seconds.

How to Access Analyst

  1. Open the Copilot app from m365.cloud.microsoft
  2. Use the left navigation to select Analyst
📎 Note: Attach your Excel files using the + button or reference files from OneDrive/SharePoint using / in your prompt. Analyst works best with structured data (tables with headers).

Prompt 1: Production Yield Analysis

Analyze this production data across our milling facilities. Identify the top 3 and bottom 3 facilities by flour extraction rate. Create a visualization comparing yield efficiency across all mills and highlight any facilities where extraction rates have declined more than 2% quarter-over-quarter. Summarize the key factors that could explain the variance.

📎 Attach: Your production data Excel file using + or /

Prompt 2: Cost Variance Analysis

Analyze this cost data comparing budget vs. actuals for our milling operations. Identify the top 3 cost categories that are over budget and the top 3 that are under budget. Create a waterfall chart showing the variance by category. Focus on wheat procurement costs, energy costs, transportation, and labor. Recommend areas for cost optimization.

📎 Attach: Budget vs. Actuals spreadsheet

Prompt 3: Scenario Modeling

Using this production and cost data, model three scenarios for next quarter: conservative (wheat prices +8%, volume flat), base case (wheat prices +3%, volume +2%), and aggressive (wheat prices -2%, volume +5%). Show a comparison chart of the impact on per-unit flour cost and total production margin for each scenario. Summarize the key assumptions.

📎 Attach: Production and cost planning spreadsheet

Additional Analyst Prompts

Analyze our energy consumption trends across milling facilities over the last 6 months. Identify which facilities have the highest energy cost per metric ton of flour produced and flag any that exceed a 10% month-over-month increase. Visualize the trends in a line chart.
Calculate key operational efficiency metrics from this data — extraction rate, energy per metric ton, downtime percentage, and on-time delivery rate. Present results in a dashboard-style table and flag any metrics that fall outside our target benchmarks.
Key Takeaway: Analyst explains its reasoning transparently so you can validate the results. It’s like having a data scientist embedded in your operations team — available on demand.

Part 5: Researcher Agent — Market Intelligence & Regulatory Research

▾
💡 Why This Matters: Ardent Mills operates at the intersection of agriculture, food manufacturing, and consumer trends. The Researcher agent can blend internal files, web data, and market intelligence into structured, well-cited deliverables — from commodity market reports to food safety regulatory updates.

What is Researcher?

Researcher is an expert agent built into the Copilot app. It handles multi-step research tasks by combining web sources with your company’s internal files and knowledge. It cites all sources so you can validate its work.

How to Access Researcher

  1. Open the Copilot app from m365.cloud.microsoft
  2. Use the left navigation to select Researcher
📎 Note: Researcher can reference internal files from OneDrive/SharePoint using /. For best results, point it to relevant operational summaries, reports, or strategy documents.
⏳ Important: Researcher prompts may take a few minutes to process because they involve multi-step reasoning. Consider submitting your first prompt early in the session so results are ready when you return to this section.

Prompt 1: Commodity Market Research

Research current wheat commodity market conditions, including hard red winter, hard red spring, and soft white wheat pricing trends over the last 6 months. Analyze the key drivers — weather patterns, global supply/demand, export activity, and USDA crop reports. Provide a summary of price outlook for the next quarter and recommend procurement timing strategies.

Prompt 2: Regulatory & Food Safety Research

Research the latest FDA food safety regulations and FSMA (Food Safety Modernization Act) updates that could impact flour milling operations in 2025–2026. Summarize key compliance requirements, any new testing or reporting obligations, and recommend preparation steps our food safety team should take. Include any updates related to pathogen control in grain-based ingredients.

Prompt 3: Competitive Intelligence

Analyze the market positioning and recent activities of [Competitor A] and [Competitor B] in the North American flour milling and grain ingredients market. Compare their product innovation strategies, sustainability initiatives, and customer segments. Present findings in a structured comparison table and highlight areas where Ardent Mills could differentiate or gain competitive advantage.

💡 Replace [Competitor A] and [Competitor B] with actual competitor names.

Key Takeaway: Researcher shows its reasoning path (“chain of thought”) and cites sources so you can trace every insight back to its origin. It accelerates strategic work that would normally take days of manual research.

🔑 Key Takeaways

🌱

Start Simple, Build Up

Begin with basic prompts and add detail as you get comfortable. Every great prompt started as a simple question.

🔗

Chain Your Tools

Use Copilot Chat to research → Analyst to model → Outlook to communicate. Chain tools for end-to-end workflows.

🔄

Iterate, Don’t Accept First Draft

Refine and follow up. Copilot gets better with each iteration — treat it as a conversation, not a one-shot answer.

🧠

AI Assists, You Decide

Copilot accelerates your work, but your milling expertise and operational judgment remain essential. Always review and validate.

IssueSolution

Draft email doesn’t include specific detailsProvide more context in your prompt — include actual numbers, facility names, and dates you want referenced.
Teams Copilot not available for a meetingMeeting transcription or recording must be enabled. Check with your IT admin if the feature is turned off.
Analyst isn’t analyzing my fileMake sure the file is a structured Excel spreadsheet with clear headers. Upload using + or reference from OneDrive using /.
Researcher is taking a long timeResearcher performs multi-step reasoning and may take 2–5 minutes for complex prompts. This is expected.
Results seem inaccurate or incompleteRefine your prompt with more specific context. Break complex questions into smaller, focused prompts.
Copilot icon not visible in Outlook or TeamsConfirm your Microsoft 365 Copilot license is active. Try the web version of the app if the desktop icon is missing.
