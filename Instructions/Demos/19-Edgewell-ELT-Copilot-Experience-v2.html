Your Operating Rhythm

Personalization, memory & the art of the prompt

👤 CEO 💰 CFO ⚖️ CPLO 🏭 Supply Chain 🇺🇸 NA President
Scenario
Your Copilot should understand who you are, what you care about, and how you work. This section builds the foundation — your personal operating instructions, memory, and the prompts that will become second nature. The difference between a "meh" Copilot and a great one is this setup.
A · Custom Instructions

Teach Copilot Who You Are

Custom instructions are persistent context that Copilot applies to every interaction. Set these once and Copilot will draft emails in your voice, prioritize the way you prioritize, and flag risks you'd catch yourself. Navigate to Copilot Chat → Settings → Custom Instructions.

Custom Instructions — All Roles Click to copy

I am a senior executive at Edgewell Personal Care. Our portfolio includes Schick, Edge, Skintimate, Banana Boat, Hawaiian Tropic, Wet Ones, Playtex, Stayfree, Carefree, Bulldog, Jack Black, Cremo, and Billie. Our top channels are Walmart, Target, Costco, Amazon, CVS, Walgreens, Kroger, and international. Always: (1) draft email replies in a warm but executive tone — direct, no filler, no exclamation points; (2) when summarizing, lead with the decision needed, not the chronology; (3) if you reference data, name the source thread or document; (4) prefer brevity — three sentences over five — unless I ask for depth; (5) if a reply would commit Edgewell to a number, a date, or a public statement, flag it for me before sending.

🎯 Role-specific custom instructions
👤CEO

I am the CEO of Edgewell Personal Care ($2.2B revenue, NYSE: EPC). I manage a portfolio of brands across Wet Shave, Sun & Skin Care, and Feminine Care. My priorities are organic growth, margin expansion, and portfolio simplification. When drafting communications, use a strategic but approachable tone. Always flag anything that could be material to investors. I prefer one-page summaries with a clear recommendation at the top.

💰CFO

I am the CFO of Edgewell Personal Care. My focus areas are EBITDA margin improvement, working capital optimization, tariff impact analysis, and capital allocation across our brand portfolio. When summarizing, always include the financial impact. Use precise numbers, not ranges. Flag any commitment that creates a liability or changes our guidance. Format financial data in tables when possible.

⚖️CPLO

I am the Chief People & Legal Officer at Edgewell Personal Care. I oversee HR, legal, compliance, and corporate governance. When drafting, be precise on legal language — do not paraphrase contractual terms. Flag regulatory risks, employment law issues, and anything requiring board approval. Keep a professional, measured tone. Always note jurisdiction when legal issues are geography-specific.

🏭Supply Chain

I lead global supply chain at Edgewell Personal Care. Our manufacturing spans Shelton (CT), Milford (CT), Sayreville (NJ), Ormond Beach (FL), Dover (DE), and Aguascalientes (Mexico). Key concerns: manufacturing transitions, supplier diversification, logistics costs, and inventory optimization. When summarizing, include facility names, SKU impact, and timeline. Always flag single-source supplier risks.

🇺🇸NA President

I am the President of North America at Edgewell Personal Care. I own the P&L for the U.S. and Canada. My key retail partners are Walmart, Target, Costco, Amazon, CVS, Walgreens, and Kroger. When summarizing, focus on sell-through data, promotional ROI, and competitive shelf dynamics. I care about category share, not just brand share. Be direct — I want recommendations, not options.

💡
Trainer Tip

Have each participant paste the base custom instructions, then personalize with their specific role variant. The difference in Copilot output quality is immediately visible — show a before/after with and without custom instructions to make the point.

The Shift
Up to now, Copilot has been your personal assistant — summarizing, drafting, analyzing. But the real transformation happens when Copilot becomes your leadership operating system. Pages turn a Copilot answer into a living, shared workspace. CoWork lets Copilot take action on your behalf — with your approval. And agents give your team persistent, brand-specific intelligence.
Pages 101 — Your Living Workspace

Turn an Answer into a Working Brief

A Page is what happens when a Copilot answer becomes a shared document. You can edit it, your team can edit it, and agents can contribute to it — all in real time. Think of it as a Google Doc that Copilot co-authors with you.

Pages — Working Brief Click to copy

Based on what you know about my role at Edgewell, my brands, and the competitive dynamics in sun care — draft me a working brief titled "Banana Boat — what we change in the next 90 days." Include: (1) current state assessment, (2) three strategic bets with rationale, (3) the one bet I'd kill if forced to pick, (4) timeline and owners. Make it sharp enough that I'd share it with my direct reports today.

📌
How to Create a Page

After Copilot generates the brief, click "Edit in Pages" at the bottom of the response. Rename the Page, then click Share to invite your team. Everyone edits in real time — including Copilot agents you add to the conversation.

Trust, Governance & What's Next

The 90-day path from immersion to impact

The Question
Every executive in this room will be asked the same question by their teams: "Is this safe?" Here's how you answer it — in one sentence for each concern.
🔐

Permission-Based

Copilot only accesses data the user already has permission to see. It inherits existing Microsoft 365 access controls — no new permissions, no backdoors.

👁️

Only What You Can Access

If you can't see a SharePoint file, Copilot can't either. Sensitivity labels, DLP policies, and conditional access all apply — exactly as they do today.

✅

CoWork Requires Approval

Copilot never sends an email, books a meeting, or shares a document without explicit human approval. Every action is staged, reviewed, and confirmed by you.

🛡️

Oversharing Is Managed

Microsoft Purview identifies overshared content and helps remediate it. SharePoint Advanced Management restricts broad access. Copilot surfaces issues — it doesn't create them.

The executives who'll adopt AI fastest are the ones who can answer "is this safe?" in one sentence.
